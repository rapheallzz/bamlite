Békwu Insights Report | 2026 Edition

The Global Black Diaspora

Culture, Streaming, and the New Attention Economy

A 59-page research report exploring how diaspora audiences influence global media consumption, streaming behavior, and cultural momentum across the United States, the Caribbean, Europe, and Africa.

Produced by Békwu Insights, the report examines how migration, identity, and digital platforms have created a cross-border ecosystem of cultural production and attention.

Why This Research Matters

Global media companies operate within an attention economy where cultural influence moves rapidly across borders and platforms.

Across music, streaming, film, and digital media, the global Black diaspora sits at the center of many of these cultural movements. Yet industry frameworks frequently treat Black audiences as a single demographic category rather than a global network shaped by migration, identity, and cross-border cultural exchange.

This report expands that perspective by examining the diaspora as a transnational media ecosystem.

Through a combination of demographic analysis, cultural observation, and media industry context, the report explores how diaspora audiences influence the direction of modern media consumption, storytelling, and platform adoption.

What the Report Covers

Together these forces illustrate the emergence of what the report describes as a diaspora attention economy, where culture, technology, and audience behavior intersect across borders.

  • Global Black diaspora population and migration patterns
  • Identity, culture, and generational worldview
  • Cross-border media movement between the United States, the United Kingdom, Africa, and the Caribbean
  • Streaming behavior across platforms including Netflix, Prime Video, YouTube, and FAST environments
  • Creator ecosystems and diaspora content production
  • Cultural economics and the measurable impact of diaspora participation

Key Insights

The research identifies several structural shifts shaping the modern media landscape.

Early Adopters

Communities with cross-border cultural exposure often discover and amplify emerging content formats before they scale to broader audiences.

Diaspora TV

For many diaspora households, YouTube functions as a hybrid media network combining film libraries, commentary programming, and creator-led content.

Economic Impact

Diaspora engagement generates significant economic impact through global music tours, cultural festivals, travel cycles, and creative industries.

Content Discovery

Media frequently circulates between the US, UK, Africa, and Caribbean, creating a transnational ecosystem of storytelling and influence.
